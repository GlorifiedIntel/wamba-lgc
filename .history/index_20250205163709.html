Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM)

The Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) is a system implemented by Wamba Local Government to enable clients to voice concerns regarding services provided by relevant Ministries, Departments, and Agencies (MDAs). It offers a platform for seeking redress when negative impacts are perceived to arise from the actions or services of these MDAs. Designed to monitor, manage, and resolve complaints effectively, the GRM specifically addresses issues raised by truckers, transporters, and traders involved in inter-state movement of goods. By establishing a structured framework for resolving trade grievances, the Local Government aims to foster fair and equitable trade practices at both local and international levels.

Submission of Trade Grievances

Any business, industry, or individual—such as traders and truckers/transporters—can submit trade grievances to the TGRM Panel regarding acts or omissions related to the collection and payment of trade-related fees and levies for inter-state goods movement.

Grievances can be submitted through the following channels:

  • Verbal Complaints: Contact the designated hotline at +234xxxxxxx.
  • Written Complaints: Send an SMS or fill out the Grievance Redress Form online.
  • Alternatively, download the form:
      Download (Docx file) Download (PDF file)

The verbal or written complaints shall detail the complainant’s name and contact, date of complaints, type of complaint, the description/nature of the grievance, relevant supporting evidence, and the desired resolution.

Resolution of Trade Grievances

The Trade Grievance Redress Mechanism (TGRM) will conduct a comprehensive review of each submitted trade grievance, engaging relevant stakeholders and experts as necessary. Following its review, the TGRM will provide recommendations for resolving the grievance. These may include negotiations with foreign governments, initiating dispute settlement proceedings, or taking other appropriate actions. The TGRM is committed to transparency and will provide regular updates to the grievance submitter on the review's progress and any proposed resolutions.

All complaints will be resolved within thirty (30) days of receipt. Appropriate actions or remedies will be recommended to address disputes in accordance with applicable laws and international agreements. The Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment will ensure that reports on trade grievance activities and resolution statuses are regularly published on the State's official website(s)

Export Strategy and Guidelines for Nasarawa State Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment

The export strategy for Nasarawa State Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment aims to enhance the State's export potential, promote local industries, and create a sustainable framework for international trade. This strategy aligns with global trade practices while leveraging Nasarawa State's unique resources and opportunities.

Export Strategy

1. Identify Priority Sectors

  • Agriculture: Sesame seeds, ginger, yams, and cashew nuts.
  • Mining and Natural Resources: Solid minerals like limestone, tin, and gemstones.
  • Handicrafts and Local Products: Unique handcrafted items and cultural artifacts.

2. Enhance Export Readiness

  • Conduct export-readiness assessments for local businesses.
  • Offer training programs on export processes, compliance, and international trade standards.
  • Facilitate certification and quality assurance programs for export goods.

3. Develop Trade Infrastructure

  • Establish export hubs and warehouses to streamline logistics.
  • Partner with stakeholders to improve transportation and shipping facilities.
  • Ensure access to reliable power and water supply in industrial zones.

4. Leverage Technology and Innovation

  • Create an online platform to showcase Nasarawa's export-ready products.
  • Utilize digital tools for market research, pricing analysis, and connecting exporters with buyers.
  • Introduce an e-trade portal for easy registration and tracking of export activities.

5. Strengthen Trade Relationships

  • Collaborate with national and international trade bodies.
  • Organize trade fairs, expos, and business delegations to promote Nasarawa's products globally.
  • Negotiate bilateral agreements with key international markets.

6. Promote Sustainable Practices

  • Encourage environmentally friendly production methods.
  • Support fair trade practices to enhance the reputation of Nasarawa’s exports.

Export Guidelines

1. Registration and Licensing

  • Businesses must register with the Nasarawa State Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment.
  • Obtain necessary export licenses and permits as per State and Federal regulations.

2. Quality Assurance

  • Ensure products meet international quality and safety standards.
  • Acquire certifications such as ISO, HACCP, or Organic Certification where applicable.

3. Documentation Requirements

  • Proforma invoice and commercial invoice.
  • Packing list, bill of lading, and certificate of origin.
  • Export declaration forms and permits.

4. Trade Financing and Incentives

  • Access financial support through export loans and credit facilities.
  • Leverage incentives like tax rebates for export-oriented industries.

5. Compliance and Legal Framework

  • Adhere to international trade laws, including WTO agreements.
  • Resolve trade disputes through the Trade Grievance Redress Mechanism (TGRM).

6. Market Research and Entry

  • Use Nasarawa’s trade data and export portals for market insights.
  • Engage in export promotion activities organized by the Ministry.

Implementation Plan

  • Short-term Goals:
    • Establish export readiness training programs.
    • Launch an online export portal.
    • Host an inaugural trade expo featuring priority sectors.
  • Mid-term Goals:
    • Build export hubs and improve trade logistics.
    • Formulate bilateral agreements with strategic markets.
  • Long-term Goals:
    • Achieve a 20% increase in Nasarawa's export revenue within five years.
    • Position Nasarawa as a top exporter of agricultural and mining products in Nigeria.

Investment Opportunities

Nasarawa State, Nigeria, offers a wealth of investment opportunities driven by its strategic location, rich natural resources, and proactive government policies aimed at fostering business growth. The state is endowed with vast agricultural land, making it a prime location for agribusiness investments, including crop farming, livestock production, and agro-processing. In addition, Nasarawa boasts abundant mineral resources such as lithium, limestone, granite, gold, and marble, offering opportunities in mining and quarrying industries.

With a growing infrastructure base and increasing urbanization, sectors such as real estate, construction, and hospitality are also ripe for investment. The state government has prioritized the development of key infrastructure, including roads, power, and water supply, to support industrial and commercial growth.

Nasarawa is also a hub for education, with several universities and institutions that provide a skilled workforce, making it an ideal location for investments in education and vocational training. With a supportive investment climate and attractive incentives for investors, Nasarawa State presents a dynamic environment for businesses seeking growth and expansion in Nigeria.
