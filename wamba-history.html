Our History

The Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment (MTII) was established following the creation of Nasarawa State on October 1, 1996, by the then Military Head of State, General Sani Abacha. Since its inception, the Ministry has been at the forefront of driving economic growth and development within the state. For nearly three decades, MTII has been entrusted with implementing and promoting industrial, commercial, investment, and cooperative development policies and strategies that align with the vision of the State Government. These efforts aim to enhance the economic well-being of Nasarawa State's citizens by fostering sustainable growth, creating opportunities, and facilitating the development of a robust economic framework.

Major Policy Thrusts

The Ministry's policy framework is guided by a comprehensive set of strategic objectives aimed at unlocking Nasarawa State's full economic potential and significantly improving the quality of life for its residents. These objectives are designed to harness the state’s abundant natural resources, human capital, and entrepreneurial spirit, fostering an environment that promotes sustainable development and inclusive growth. By aligning its policies with global best practices and state-specific needs, the Ministry seeks to drive innovation, create jobs, and ensure equitable access to economic opportunities across all sectors. The major policy thrusts include:

1
Optimum Utilization of Resources:

MTII is committed to ensuring the efficient and effective use of the state’s natural, human, and financial resources to achieve sustainable economic growth and development.

2
Rural Industrialization through Cooperative Initiatives:

The Ministry focuses on stimulating industrial activities in rural areas by leveraging the cooperative approach and establishing catalytic projects. These initiatives aim to unlock the economic potential of rural communities and reduce urban migration.

3
Promoting Private Sector Participation:

MTII actively creates opportunities for private sector engagement in the state’s economic development. By fostering a business-friendly environment, the Ministry encourages investment, innovation, and entrepreneurship to drive job creation and economic empowerment for the people of Nasarawa State.

Through its history and strategic focus, the Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment remains a vital driver of Nasarawa State’s transformation into a thriving hub of trade, industry, and investment.
